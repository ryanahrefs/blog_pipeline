# Ahrefs Content Gap Tool Overview

**Source**: https://ahrefs.com/content-gap

The Content Gap tool in Ahrefs Site Explorer helps you find keywords that your competitors rank for but you don't. This is the primary tool for keyword gap analysis in Ahrefs.

## How to use the Content Gap tool

1. Go to Site Explorer
2. Enter your domain
3. Click "Content Gap" in the left sidebar
4. Add up to 10 competing domains
5. Click "Show keywords"

The tool returns a list of keywords that at least one of the competitors ranks for in the top 10, but you don't rank for at all (or rank poorly).

## Key features

- **Filter by position**: Show only keywords where competitors rank in top 10, top 5, or top 3
- **Intersection filters**: Show keywords where 1+ competitors rank, 2+ competitors rank, or all competitors rank
- **Keyword filters**: Filter by volume, KD, traffic potential, word count
- **Export**: Download full keyword list for further analysis

## Best practices from Ahrefs

- Start with 3-5 direct competitors
- Use "All" intersection filter to find keywords ALL competitors rank for (strongest signals)
- Filter by KD < 30 to find achievable opportunities
- Check parent topic to group related keywords
- Verify search intent matches your content capabilities

# How to Do a Content Gap Analysis

**Source**: https://ahrefs.com/blog/content-gap-analysis/

## What is content gap analysis?

Content gap analysis is the process of finding topics your competitors have covered that you haven't. It reveals keyword opportunities and content ideas you're missing.

## Two types of content gaps

1. **Domain-level gaps**: Competitors have content about topics you don't cover at all
2. **Page-level gaps**: You and a competitor both have a page about the same topic, but they rank for more keywords

## Step-by-step process

### Step 1: Identify competitors

- Enter your "money" keywords in Google
- Note which domains consistently rank in top positions
- Include 3-5 direct competitors in your analysis

### Step 2: Run the Competitive Analysis

In Ahrefs:
1. Go to Site Explorer > Competitive Analysis
2. Enter your domain and 1-3 competing domains
3. Click "Show keyword opportunities"

### Step 3: Analyze the results

The tool shows keywords your competitors rank for that you don't. For each keyword, you'll see:
- Search volume
- Keyword difficulty
- Traffic potential
- Which competitors rank (and their positions)

### Step 4: Filter and prioritize

Not every gap is worth pursuing. Filter by:
- **Volume**: Focus on keywords with meaningful search volume
- **Difficulty**: Prioritize achievable keywords (KD < 40)
- **Relevance**: Ensure keywords match your business

### Step 5: Create or optimize content

For each prioritized gap:
- Check if you have existing content to optimize
- If not, create new content targeting the keyword
- Analyze top-ranking pages to understand required depth

# SEO Competitor Analysis Methodology

**Source**: https://ahrefs.com/blog/seo-competitor-analysis/

## Finding competitors for gap analysis

Your SEO competitors may differ from your business competitors. To find them:

1. **Search your main keywords**: Who ranks consistently?
2. **Use Ahrefs' Organic Competitors report**: Shows domains competing for the same keywords
3. **Check intersection**: Look for domains that rank for many of your target keywords

## Competitive metrics to consider

When evaluating competitors and gap opportunities:

- **Domain Rating (DR)**: Overall backlink strength
- **Organic traffic**: Total estimated search traffic
- **Number of keywords**: Keyword footprint size
- **Top pages**: What content drives their traffic

## Action steps after finding gaps

1. **Group keywords by topic**: Use parent topic to cluster related terms
2. **Map to content types**: Are these blog posts, product pages, or tools?
3. **Assess feasibility**: Can you create better content than what ranks?
4. **Plan content calendar**: Schedule gap-filling content strategically
5. **Track progress**: Monitor keyword positions after publishing
