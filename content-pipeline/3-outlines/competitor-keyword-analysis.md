## What Is Competitor Keyword Analysis?
**Target**: ~250 words

- **BLUF**: Competitor keyword analysis is the process of discovering which keywords your competitors rank for—and using that intelligence to inform your own SEO strategy.
- It answers: What's working for them? What are they missing? Where can I compete?
- Different from general keyword research: starts with competitor performance, not seed keywords
- The output: a prioritized list of keyword opportunities based on competitor success and your ability to compete
- **Evidence**: Mention that most successful SEO campaigns start with competitive intelligence, not brainstorming

## How to Identify Your Real SEO Competitors
**Target**: ~350 words

- **BLUF**: Your SEO competitors aren't always your business competitors—they're whoever ranks for the keywords you want.
- Business competitors vs SEO competitors: a SaaS company might compete with blogs, not just other SaaS
- Method 1: Search your target keywords and note who consistently appears
- Method 2: Use Site Explorer's "Competing Domains" report to find sites with keyword overlap
- Method 3: Check who ranks for your brand-adjacent terms
- Focus on 3-5 competitors to keep analysis manageable
- **Evidence**: Example—a project management tool might compete more with Asana's blog than with Monday.com for content keywords

## Find Your Competitors' Keywords (The Extraction Process)
**Target**: ~500 words

- **BLUF**: The fastest way to find competitor keywords is to pull their entire organic keyword profile and filter for opportunities.

### Using Site Explorer for Single Competitor Analysis
- Enter competitor domain → Organic Keywords report
- Filter by position (top 10 or top 20) to see their winners
- Sort by traffic to find their money keywords
- Export for further analysis

### Using Content Gap for Multi-Competitor Analysis
- Enter your domain + up to 10 competitors
- See keywords they rank for that you don't
- This reveals the exact gaps in your content strategy
- Filter by volume, difficulty, or traffic potential

### What to Extract
- Keywords they rank for (positions 1-20)
- Their top pages by traffic
- Keywords where they rank but you don't (gaps)
- **Evidence**: Show specific filters—traffic > 100, KD < 50, positions 1-10

## Analyze and Prioritize Your Findings
**Target**: ~600 words

- **BLUF**: Raw keyword lists are useless—you need a framework to prioritize opportunities by potential impact and your ability to rank.

### The Business Value Framework (0-3 Scale)
- Score 3: Keyword directly relates to your product/service
- Score 2: Keyword is relevant but broader
- Score 1: Tangentially related
- Score 0: No clear business connection
- Why business value beats volume: 100 searches from buyers > 10,000 from browsers

### Combine Metrics for Prioritization
- Business value × Traffic potential ÷ Keyword difficulty = Priority score
- Quick wins: Low KD + high business value + decent volume
- Long-term plays: High volume + high difficulty (need content + links)

### Identify Different Opportunity Types
- **Gap keywords**: They rank, you don't—content creation opportunities
- **Overlap keywords**: Both rank, but they're higher—content improvement opportunities
- **Weak competitor pages**: Low DR pages ranking = beatable with better content
- **Evidence**: Example prioritization spreadsheet with scored keywords

## Turn Competitor Insights Into a Content Plan
**Target**: ~450 words

- **BLUF**: The goal isn't just a list of keywords—it's a content roadmap that systematically captures competitor traffic.

### Map Keywords to Content Types
- Informational keywords → blog posts, guides
- Commercial keywords → comparison pages, product content
- Cluster related keywords to target multiple with single pages

### Create Your Content Calendar
- Prioritize quick wins first for early momentum
- Schedule high-difficulty targets with supporting content strategy
- Plan content upgrades for overlap keywords

### Track Progress Against Competitors
- Monitor rank changes for target keywords
- Track traffic growth vs competitor baseline
- Set quarterly goals for keyword capture
- **Evidence**: Mention Rank Tracker for ongoing competitive monitoring

## Advanced Tactics: Go Beyond Basic Analysis
**Target**: ~400 words

- **BLUF**: The best opportunities often hide in the edges—competitor weaknesses, emerging trends, and underexplored angles.

### Find Weak Competitor Pages
- Sort competitor keywords by URL
- Identify pages with low referring domains but good rankings
- These are vulnerable to better, more comprehensive content

### Analyze Competitor Content Gaps
- Look for keywords where multiple competitors rank but with thin content
- Find questions competitors don't answer well
- Check for outdated competitor content (3+ years old)

### Consider AI Overview Impact
- Some keywords now have significant AI Overview presence
- Factor in click behavior when AI provides direct answers
- Prioritize keywords where organic clicks remain high
- **Evidence**: Reference SERP analysis showing AI Overview features

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
**Target**: ~250 words

- **BLUF**: Most competitor analysis fails not from lack of data, but from lack of execution discipline.
- Mistake 1: Analyzing too many competitors (stick to 3-5)
- Mistake 2: Chasing high-volume keywords without business value
- Mistake 3: Copying competitor strategy instead of finding gaps
- Mistake 4: One-time analysis instead of ongoing monitoring
- Mistake 5: Ignoring your own strengths and unique angles
- **Evidence**: The goal is differentiation, not duplication
