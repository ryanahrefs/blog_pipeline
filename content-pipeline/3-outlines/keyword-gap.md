## What is a keyword gap?
**Target**: ~250 words

- **BLUF**: A keyword gap is a keyword that your competitors rank for but you don't. It represents traffic you're missing out on.
- The simplest way to think about it: if Competitor A ranks for "content marketing strategy" and you don't appear anywhere in the top 100, that's a keyword gap
- Also called "keyword gap analysis" or sometimes confused with "content gap" (they're related but different - more on that below)
- **Evidence**: Example showing a hypothetical gap - Competitor ranks #3 for "SEO audit checklist" (1,500 monthly searches), you rank nowhere

### Keyword gap vs content gap

- **BLUF**: Keyword gap = missing keywords; Content gap = missing topics/content pieces
- Keyword gap: You don't rank for specific search terms
- Content gap: You're missing entire content pieces or topics that competitors cover
- In practice, fixing keyword gaps often means creating content, so the terms get used interchangeably
- Ahrefs uses "Content Gap" as the tool name, but it finds keyword gaps

## How to find keyword gaps with Ahrefs
**Target**: ~600 words

- **BLUF**: Use Ahrefs' Content Gap tool to find keywords all (or most) of your competitors rank for that you don't.

### Step 1: Identify your SEO competitors

- Your SEO competitors aren't always your business competitors
- Search your main target keywords and note who consistently appears
- Use Ahrefs Site Explorer > Organic Competitors report for data-driven competitor list
- Pick 3-5 direct competitors (more dilutes results)
- **Evidence**: Example of entering a domain and seeing top organic competitors

### Step 2: Run the Content Gap analysis

- Go to Site Explorer > Content Gap
- Enter your domain as the target
- Add competitor domains (up to 10)
- Click "Show keywords"
- **Evidence**: Screenshot placeholder showing the Content Gap interface

### Step 3: Filter the results

- Use "Intersect" filter to show keywords where 2+ or all competitors rank
- Keywords all competitors rank for = strongest opportunities
- Filter by position (top 10 only) to focus on keywords competitors actually win
- Set minimum volume threshold (100+) to avoid low-value terms
- Add KD filter (< 30) to find achievable opportunities

### Step 4: Export and analyze

- Export the keyword list for deeper analysis
- Group by parent topic to see content themes
- Check search intent for each keyword cluster
- **Evidence**: Example showing a filtered export with 50-100 keyword opportunities

## How to prioritize keyword gap opportunities
**Target**: ~400 words

- **BLUF**: Not every gap is worth filling - prioritize based on traffic potential, difficulty, and business relevance.

### The three-filter framework

1. **Traffic potential over volume**: Traffic potential accounts for the total traffic the ranking page can get from all keywords, not just one
2. **Difficulty that matches your authority**: If your DR is 40, targeting KD 70+ keywords is unrealistic short-term
3. **Business relevance**: Some high-volume keywords might not align with what you sell

### Quick wins vs strategic bets

- **Quick wins (pursue first)**:
  - High traffic potential + low difficulty + high relevance
  - Keywords where you already have related content to expand
  - Exact-match topics to your products/services

- **Strategic bets (plan for later)**:
  - High traffic potential + higher difficulty
  - Keywords requiring new content types (tools, calculators, guides)
  - Topics where you'd need to build topical authority first

### Don't ignore intent

- Informational keywords need blog content
- Commercial keywords need product/comparison pages
- Transactional keywords need landing pages
- **Evidence**: Example showing a keyword gap with commercial intent that requires a different approach than a blog post

## What to do with your keyword gaps
**Target**: ~400 words

- **BLUF**: Finding gaps is useless without action - here's how to turn gaps into rankings.

### Option 1: Optimize existing content

- Check if you have content that should rank but doesn't
- Search your site: `site:yourdomain.com "keyword"`
- If existing content exists, it might need optimization rather than new content
- Add missing subtopics, update outdated info, improve depth

### Option 2: Create new content

- For gaps where you have no relevant content
- Analyze top-ranking pages to understand:
  - Content format (guide, list, tool)
  - Word count and depth
  - Subtopics covered
  - Unique angles to differentiate

### Option 3: Build supporting content

- Some keywords require topical authority to rank
- Create cluster content around the main topic
- Internal link strategically to boost the target page

### Track your progress

- Add gap keywords to Ahrefs Rank Tracker
- Monitor rankings after publishing/updating content
- Re-run gap analysis quarterly to find new opportunities
- **Evidence**: Example of tracking keyword movements over 3 months after filling a gap

## Common keyword gap analysis mistakes
**Target**: ~250 words

- **BLUF**: Most people fail at gap analysis not because they can't find gaps, but because they target the wrong ones.

### Mistake 1: Targeting every gap

- Not all gaps are opportunities - some are gaps for good reason
- You can't rank for everything; focus beats breadth

### Mistake 2: Ignoring search intent

- Finding a gap keyword doesn't mean your existing content can rank for it
- Match content type to intent, or you'll never rank

### Mistake 3: Not considering your authority

- A DR 30 site targeting DR 90 keywords is fighting uphill
- Build authority progressively

### Mistake 4: Running gap analysis once

- Competitor strategies change
- New keywords emerge
- Run quarterly at minimum

### Mistake 5: Only looking at direct competitors

- Sometimes the best gaps come from adjacent industries
- A SaaS company might find gaps by analyzing content marketing agencies

## FAQ (optional, if word count allows)
**Target**: ~150 words

### What is the best tool for keyword gap analysis?

- Ahrefs, Semrush, and Moz all offer keyword gap tools
- Ahrefs' Content Gap tool is integrated directly into Site Explorer, making competitor analysis seamless

### How often should I run keyword gap analysis?

- Quarterly at minimum
- Monthly if you're in a fast-moving industry
- After any major competitor content launches

### Can I do keyword gap analysis for free?

- Ahrefs Webmaster Tools offers limited competitive data for your own site
- For full competitor gap analysis, a paid tool is typically necessary
